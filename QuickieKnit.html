One-Piece Quickie Knit

“A Fashion Sweater in Medium Size”

 

Pattern source The Illinois Yarn Company

Date late 1950s-early 1960s

Materials

  • Link to pattern (PDF)
  • Worsted weight yarn, approximately 10 ounces
  • Knitting needles size 6 and 11
  • Crochet hook size G-H
  • Buttons: Pattern photo shows 5 x 1-inch buttons, I used 6 x ¾-inch buttons

Notes and modifications

  • Pattern is written in one “medium” size. Mine measured approx. 38” bust, 28” waist (unstretched), 17” long.
  • Gauge was not given in the pattern. Mine is 13 stitches and 17 rows in 4” in stockinette.
  • Yarn used here: Lion Brand Wool Ease Heathers (aran weight). Total yarn usage was 3 skeins = 591 yards (540.4 meters), 9 ounces (255 grams).
  • The pattern was written for Rochelle Knitting Worsted, a 100% wool yarn, and although it wasn’t explicitly stated in the pattern, they likely expected the knitter would block before finishing and wearing. If you use synthetic fibers and/or don’t block, you will likely want to add length to both the body and sleeves.
  • Construction and shaping: This is a loosely-knit, waist-length, ¾-sleeved cardigan, worked top down, with a mostly circular yoke. Sleeves are worked from live stitches, knitted flat and seamed. (You could do them in the round.) There is no body shaping, and the only sleeve shaping is done at the cuff, so both body and sleeves can be worked to any length. Button band is single crochet.
  • Speaking of that button band, instructions aren’t really included in the pattern. You want it to be a little on the short side; it should “pull in” the loose knitting and keep it from sagging longer in front. I used an H hook (I’m a tight crocheter) and winged it - mine ended up as 46 stitches picked up evenly.
  • Buttonholes were simply [ch 1, skip 1 st, sc in next st] on row 3, then sc in the ch-1 space on row 4.
  • I did not math out evenly-spaced buttonholes; I marked placement (eyeballed) with safety pins and worked accordingly. They aren’t perfectly even and I don’t care. 😂
  • Could you pick up stitches and knit the button band instead? I didn’t try this, but I imagine you could use size 6 needles and work seed stitch for 6-8 rows (1 inch total). Work buttonholes as [yo, k2tog] in desired locations.
  • Minor mods: 
  • I used a mostly-synthetic yarn and did not block, so I added 2” to the body length and 3” to the sleeve length.  
  • I did the collar ribbing on 6’s and found it a bit rigid for my taste, so I switched to 7’s for the cuffs and 8’s for the hem.  (I didn’t swatch - can you tell?)
