One-Piece Quickie Knit (Cardigan)

Pattern source The Illinois Yarn Company

Date late 1950s-early 1960s

Materials

  • Link to pattern (PDF)
  • Worsted weight yarn, approximately 10 ounces (I used 9 ounces of Wool Ease)
  • Knitting needles size 6 and 11
  • Crochet hook size G
  • Buttons: 5 (1 inch) or 6 (¾ inch)

Notes and modifications

  1. This is a loosely-knit, waist-length, ¾-sleeved cardigan, pattern written in one “medium” size. Mine measured approx. 38” bust, 28” waist (unstretched), 17” long.
  2. Gauge was not given in the pattern. Mine is 13 stitches and 17 rows in 4” in stockinette. Row gauge isn’t important.
  3. Yarn choice matters. I used a mostly-synthetic yarn (Wool Ease) and did not block, so I added 2” to the body length and 3” to the sleeve length. If you use wool and plan to block, I recommend swatching to see how much your knit will grow.
  4. Construction: This is one-piece from the top down. Sleeves are knitted flat and seamed. (You could do them in the round, but I opted to follow the pattern.) Button band is single crochet.
  5. Minor mods: I added length to both body and sleeves, as noted above. I also did the waist ribbing on larger needles (size 8) for a looser fit. I don’t have a girdled 1950s waist, after all. 😂
